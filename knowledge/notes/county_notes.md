## Drought phases (ASAL counties)

Kenya's National Drought Management Authority classifies arid and semi-arid
counties into phases: Normal, Alert, Alarm, Emergency, and Recovery, based on
environmental, production, access, and utilization indicators. In early 2026,
counties including Mandera, Wajir, Kilifi, and Kwale were classified in the
Alarm phase, while Turkana, Marsabit, Samburu, Isiolo, Baringo, Laikipia,
Tharaka Nithi, Kajiado, Taita Taveta, Kitui, Tana River, Garissa, and Lamu were
in the Alert phase. (Source: NDMA National Drought Early Warning Bulletin, 2026.)

## Tana River flooding

The Tana River regularly bursts its banks during the long rains (March–May),
with flood risk heightened when the upstream Seven Forks dams are full.
Settlements in Garissa, Lamu, and Tana River counties are repeatedly exposed.
Flooding in the lower basin damages crops, livestock, and infrastructure, and is
associated with outbreaks of waterborne disease such as cholera. (Sources:
ACAPS Kenya Floods briefing, 2024; NDMA.)

In Madogo Ward (Tana River County), studies record flooding roughly every two
years, with topography (low-lying land near the river) as the dominant
vulnerability factor; communities cope mainly by relocating to higher ground.
(Source: Odoyo et al., 2024, CC-BY 4.0.)

## Population exposure and equity

A large share of the population in the lower Tana basin lives in flood-prone
households because livelihoods depend on the river for farming and water.
Vulnerability is unevenly distributed — differentiated by age, gender, and
disability — which is why ranking counties by physical hazard alone is
insufficient and a transparent, exposure-aware risk score is used here.
(Sources: Tana River flood-governance and vulnerability literature; see
`sources.md`.)

## Coastal and western flooding

Coastal counties (Mombasa, Kilifi, Kwale) experience flash flooding from intense
short rains. In western Kenya, Budalangi (Busia County) is among the most
flood-prone areas due to the overflow of the Nzoia River. (Source: NDMA;
regional flood records.)
